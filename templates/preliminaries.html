Please review the 5 criteria below before evaluating the poems.

1. Clarity

Clarity indicates whether the language aligns with the mood and tone of the poem. Generally, higher clarity is indicative of a higher quality poem.

2. Literary Devices

These are techniques poets use to add depth, meaning, and style beyond the literal meaning.

Metaphor Comparison between unlike things (no "like"/"as") to highlight a shared quality.
Simile Comparison using "like" or "as" (e.g., "brave as a lion").
Alliteration Repetition of the same consonant sound at the start of words.
Imagery Language appealing to senses to create vivid pictures.
Personification Giving human qualities to inanimate objects.
Foreshadowing Hinting at future events to create suspense.
Allusion Indirect reference to a person, place, event, or art.
Symbolism Using an object/idea to represent a deeper meaning.
Juxtaposition Placing two contrasting things side-by-side.

3. Punctuation & Spacing

These are indicative of structure. A quality poem usually maintains a uniform structure.

Note: Poems can be unstructured if intended, but the message must remain clear despite the structure.

4. Grammar & Spelling

The usage of grammar and spelling must be accurate throughout the work.

5. Originality

Themes must be original and not rehashed from pop culture, clich√©s, or other prominent works.

Additionally, the mood must remain constant throughout the poem unless a shift is clearly intended by the poet.
